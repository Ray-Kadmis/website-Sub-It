Vehicle Mechanic

A vehicle mechanic has many responsibilities. You must diagnose vehicle problems, check for engine problems, brake malfunctions, and electrical issues, and repair and replace parts once the problem is identified. This job requires you to be quite vigilant.

Essential Responsibilities

Every vehicle mechanic has many responsibilities. These responsibilities must be fulfilled dutifully so that the vehicles function correctly. Below are some of the essential duties of air vehicle mechanics.

  • You have to perform organisational and intermediate-level maintenance, modify military equipment and subsystems, ensure that all support equipment related to 25, 10, or 20-ton trucks is functioning correctly, and ensure that the cranes, pumps, and compressors work seamlessly. You also need a wide assortment of wheels to Replace old tyres.
  • You must be able to diagnose various system malfunctions and isolate the causes. You must also correct any deficiencies in the vehicle.
  • You will also be asked to dismantle and reassemble equipment independently or if you are confused by using the technical manuals. It includes dismantling and reassembling lifting devices and hand and pneumatic tools.
  • You will be asked to perform inspection and repairs and replace components, engines, transmissions, and sub-assemblies.
  • One of your core responsibilities is training team members to solve unusual maintenance problems. You will teach them how to recognise and troubleshoot various issues. You will also have to identify parts in repair manuals and request an order for those parts if needed.
  • You will also have to direct workers in cleaning parts and assembling various equipment. You may have to adjust and maintain machinery such as stripping and loading shovels. This machinery will also include drilling and cutting machines and related equipment.
  • You will also recommend revisions to maintenance procedures to improve efficiency. You will also have to ensure quality artistry.

Work environment and physical demands

The work you will perform will be in a shop, and the environment will be moderately light. It is sometimes heated and ventilated, exposing you to disagreeable fumes. You may also have face cuts and bruises from using tools in closed spaces.

You will also face the hazards of moving vehicles such as forklifts and sweepers in the shop area. Keep in mind that while performing the duties of this job, you will be regularly required to stand and use your hands. You will also use your fingers to handle or feel various equipment. You will frequently need to walk and reach with your hands and arms.

You will also be required to stoop, kneel, crouch, and even crawl. You will have to work in tiring and uncomfortable positions, so this is a physically demanding field. You will occasionally be required to sit, climb, or balance as an employee. Your job may even require you to taste or smell to ensure everything is functioning correctly with the vehicle.

You will frequently lift items weighing at least 40 to 45 lbs, which can even increase to more than 50 lbs. You will be exposed to moving mechanical parts, extreme heat, and vibration. The employees will occasionally be exposed to wet and humid conditions because they might have to work in highly precarious places. They will work in fumes or airborne particles, outside weather conditions, and extreme cold.

There is also the risk of electrical shocks, so keep that in mind. The noise level in your work environment will usually be loud, and you may be working in unsafe conditions. You will also question nonstandard operations over environmental factors involving unmitigated hazards. You will provide your feedback to the supervisors and managers on all safety issues to ensure improvement in the workspace. You will also perform preventive maintenance checks and services on equipment and report any faults if you find any. You must be able to speak, read, correct and understand English.

Truck mechanics

Truck mechanics, also known as diesel services technicians, perform various tasks on heavy-duty trucks, giant construction vehicles, buses, and tractor-trailers. As a truck mechanic, you will inspect, diagnose, and repair any brake system. You will also diagnose and fix any engine, electrical components, and steering system issues. This routine also involves checking the transmission and the tyres for any problems.

As a truck mechanic, you will work for general and specialised freight companies, bus companies, various truck manufacturers, and local government agencies. Truck mechanic job responsibilities

A truck mechanic job has many responsibilities, and you can get started on this job if you can complete the following responsibilities.

  • Perform routine truck maintenance, including checking batteries and changing oil to ensure proper vehicle running.
  • Inspect various parts of the trucks, including the braking system, wheel bearings, and steering mechanisms. It is done to ensure that they are in proper condition to operate effectively.
  • You must use different tools and equipment. You commonly use pressure gauges, pneumatic wrenches, wells, ENT, and screwdrivers. You will also use hoists and jacks to repair trucks while constantly observing safety precautions.
  • You must diagnose various malfunctions and troubleshoot problems in defective equipment. You will do this by inspecting and testing defective equipment using motor analysers. You will also use handheld computers and chassis charts to determine the best plan of attack to address these issues.
  • You also have to test-drive vehicles to ensure they are functioning correctly and find issues to determine the solution to apply.
  • You also have to replace parts, such as valves and bearings. You may also need to remove and replace gears and repair vehicle cooling and heating systems.
  • You will measure vehicle emissions to ensure they are within the proper limits.
  • Ensure that every part conforms to this factory's specifications by inspecting and verifying clearances and dimensions.
  • Truck mechanic job specifications
  • To be a truck mechanic, you must fulfill some specifications. These specifications include the following.
  • You must have completed a standard mechanic certification program from a reputable agency.
  • You must have at least two years of experience working as a mechanic.
  • To work in this environment, you must be physically strong, have good grip strength, and be agile and flexible.
  • You must have essential critical thinking, analysis, and evaluation skills.

  • You must always be present on time and be well-rested before starting your shift.
  • You must listen and be a team player to progress in your career.
  • You have to learn new technology to create innovative solutions.
  • You must have proper training and certification from a reputable agency.
  • You must have good computer skills because you will use them to diagnose and troubleshoot problems.
